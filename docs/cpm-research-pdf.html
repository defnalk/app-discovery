Headline: the delta thesis holds — but it flips where the money is. A UGC asset costs roughly the same everywhere marketplaces operate, while paid CPMs vary 12x across our markets. The dollar value of substituting UGC for paid is therefore largest where paid is most expensive — the US, led by health & fitness ($20.70 CPM, +38% YoY) — and compresses or inverts in cheap-paid markets unless local creator rates are proportionally 4–12x cheaper. That local-rate variable is unverified and is exactly what decides whether Turkey/Brazil/Mexico beat the US. Collecting it (Bullet + local creator marketplaces) is the highest-leverage next step.

The delta, honestly stated

The "~12x paid-vs-UGC delta" could not be directly verified: no organic-reach-per-post data survived fact-checking, so true UGC effective CPMs aren't computable from public sources. (The only verified ~12x is the US-vs-India paid spread.) What verified inputs do support is break-even arithmetic:

A $197 UGC asset beats paid when it reaches…Impressions
US health & fitness ($20.70 CPM)~9,500
US baseline ($16.08)~12,250
Mexico ($3.92)~50,000
Brazil ($2.63)~75,000
India ($1.36)~145,000

→ At US creator prices, pool markets need 4–12x the reach to break even. They become attractive only at local creator rates — e.g. a $20–40 Turkish creator asset would match or beat US economics. That number is the research gap.

Opportunity ranking — market × industry (provisional)

  1. US × health & fitness — largest verified paid denominator ($20.70, fastest-inflating)
  2. US × e-commerce D2C — $13–14 CPMs, deepest UGC supply, best data coverage
  3. US × fintech / edtech / AI tools / entertainment — assume near the $14–16 baseline (vertical CPMs unverified; fintech likely above)
  4. Mexico & Brazil — local-creator supply arbitrage (not raw CPM delta); MX has the highest paid CPM of the pool markets
  5. Turkey & Indonesia — same arbitrage logic, thinnest data; TR possibly the cheapest creator supply of all (unverified)
  6. India — $1.36 paid CPM is the lowest bar for UGC to beat; pencils only with very cheap local creators

Data gaps → next research steps

  1. Local creator rate cards for TR/BR/MX/IN/ID via Bullet + Creatorden/Squid/Qoruz/SociaBuzz — the single variable that decides pool-market vs US priority.
  2. App-install campaign CPMs — all verified panels are e-commerce-weighted; spot-check 10–15 known consumer apps per market in Meta Ads Library.
  3. Organic reach per creator post by market (Bullet) — converts creator fees into effective CPMs and turns break-even arithmetic into the true delta ratio.
