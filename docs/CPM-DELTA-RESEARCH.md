# CPM delta research — paid vs UGC, by market × industry (preliminary)

*Delivered 2026-06-12 (a day ahead of the Saturday deadline). 103-agent web research with adversarial verification — every number below survived 3-vote fact-checking unless marked otherwise; refuted numbers were discarded, not softened. Sources: Triple Whale (~35k Shopify brands), Lebesgue 51-country table (Mar 2026), Gupta Media, Adligator, Collabstr 2025 payouts.*

## Executive summary

**The verified data supports the delta thesis — but flips where the money is.** A UGC asset costs roughly the same everywhere marketplaces operate (~$154–197), while paid CPMs vary 12x across our markets. So the *dollar value* of substituting UGC for paid is largest where paid is most expensive — the **US, especially health & fitness ($20.70 CPM, +38% YoY)** — and *compresses or inverts* in cheap-paid markets like India ($1.36 CPM) **unless local creator rates are proportionally 4–12x cheaper, which is exactly the unverified variable.** That makes local creator rate-card collection (Bullet + TR/BR local marketplaces) the highest-leverage research left, not more paid benchmarks.

Structural tailwind regardless of market: paid CPMs inflated +16–20% YoY in 2025 while UGC asset costs *deflated* −5.7%.

## UGC cost baseline (verified)

- Creator asset (US-dominated marketplaces, 2025 actual payouts): **UGC $154, TikTok $186, IG $193**; average campaign $197, **down 5.7% YoY**.
- Nano/micro TikTok tiers: ~$50–200 / $200–800 per post.
- Supply: marketplace UGC is 66% US-concentrated — but that's platform selection, not scarcity. Brazil (~3.8M creators), India, Indonesia have huge creator bases on *local* platforms (Creatorden TR, Squid/Influency.me BR, Qoruz IN, SociaBuzz ID). **The emerging-market play is supply aggregation/arbitrage, not paying scarcity prices.**

## The delta, honestly stated

The hypothesized "12x paid-vs-UGC delta" **could not be directly verified** — no organic-reach-per-post data survived fact-checking, so effective UGC CPMs can't be computed from public data. (The only verified ~12x is the US-vs-India *paid* spread.) What the verified inputs do support is break-even arithmetic:

| A $197 UGC asset beats paid when it reaches… | Impressions needed |
|---|---|
| US health & fitness ($20.70 CPM) | **~9,500** |
| US baseline ($16.08) | ~12,250 |
| Mexico ($3.92) | ~50,000 |
| Brazil ($2.63) | ~75,000 |
| India ($1.36) | ~145,000 |

→ At *US creator prices*, emerging markets need 4–12x the organic reach to break even. The TR/BR/MX opportunity exists **only at local creator rates** — if a TR creator costs $20–40/asset (plausible, unverified), Turkey's economics match or beat the US. **This is the number to nail down with Bullet + local rate cards.**

## Provisional opportunity ranking (market × industry)

1. **US × health & fitness** — biggest verified paid denominator ($20.70, fastest-inflating +38% YoY)
2. **US × e-commerce D2C** — $13–14 CPMs, deepest UGC supply, best benchmark coverage
3. **US × fintech / edtech / AI tools / entertainment** — assume near $14–16 baseline (fintech likely above; vertical CPMs unverified)
4. **Mexico & Brazil** — via local-creator supply arbitrage (not raw CPM delta); MX has the highest paid CPM of the pool markets
5. **Turkey & Indonesia** — same arbitrage logic, thinner data; TR possibly cheapest creator supply of all (unverified)
6. **India** — $1.36 paid CPM sets the lowest bar for UGC to beat; needs very cheap local creators to pencil

## Data gaps → Saturday follow-ups (the manual slice)

1. **Local creator rate cards** for TR/BR/MX/IN/ID — via Bullet social listening + Creatorden/Squid/Qoruz/SociaBuzz. This is the single variable that decides whether pool markets beat the US.
